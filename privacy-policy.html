Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 8 April 2026

ShoreVest Partners, Ltd., together with its affiliates, subsidiaries, associated undertakings, representative offices, related funds, general partners, management entities and other entities operating under or in connection with the ShoreVest name, as applicable in the relevant context, are referred to in this Privacy Policy collectively as “ShoreVest”, “we”, “us” or “our”.

ShoreVest recognises the importance of protecting personal information and is committed to processing personal information responsibly, lawfully and with appropriate regard to confidentiality, security and applicable data protection requirements. This Privacy Policy describes how ShoreVest collects, records, organises, stores, uses, shares, transfers and otherwise processes personal information in connection with this website, any investor-access or restricted-access sections of this website, and certain related online and offline business interactions that refer or relate to this Privacy Policy.

This Privacy Policy is intended primarily for professional, institutional and business audiences. It is not drafted as a mass-market consumer privacy notice. Different or additional notices may apply in specific contexts, including investor onboarding, fund subscriptions, employment, recruitment, contractual relationships, physical office visits, events, service-provider arrangements or other dealings with ShoreVest. Where another notice or contractual provision applies to a specific interaction, that more specific notice or provision may supplement or prevail over this Privacy Policy to the extent of any inconsistency.

By accessing or using this website, submitting information through it, requesting access to restricted materials, corresponding with ShoreVest in connection with the website, or otherwise providing personal information where this Privacy Policy applies, you acknowledge the practices described in this Privacy Policy, subject always to your rights under applicable law.

1. Scope of this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to personal information processed by or on behalf of ShoreVest in connection with:

  • this public website and any pages, features, forms, publications, downloads, contact tools or functionalities made available through it
  • any password-protected, investor-access, portal, data room, document-sharing, diligence, reporting, event or restricted-access sections or services made available by or on behalf of ShoreVest
  • communications arising from or connected to the website, including contact requests, investor enquiries, business development correspondence, media or press enquiries, meeting requests, subscription-related interactions and similar exchanges
  • business relationship management, investor-access administration, due diligence activity, compliance review, operational support and internal record-keeping linked to website interactions or website-referred relationships
  • recruitment, talent, career and hiring-related interactions where applications or enquiries are made through this website or through channels specifically linked from it

This Privacy Policy does not apply to:

  • websites, platforms, portals, communication tools or services operated by third parties and not controlled by ShoreVest, even if linked from this website
  • third-party providers whose privacy practices are governed by their own privacy notices, terms or contractual arrangements
  • employment and internal workforce matters except to the extent expressly stated in a particular context
  • fund-specific subscription, onboarding, investor servicing or transaction-specific arrangements where a more specific privacy notice or contract applies
  • purely anonymised information that no longer relates to an identified or identifiable person under applicable law

This Privacy Policy should be read together with any Cookie Notice, Terms of Use, legal notices, subscription documentation, non-disclosure agreements, side letters, application forms and other terms or disclosures that may apply to a particular relationship or interaction with ShoreVest.

2. Personal information we may collect

Depending on the nature of your interaction with ShoreVest, we may collect and process a range of personal information, including the categories described below.

2.1 Professional and contact details

We may collect professional identification and contact information, including your name, title, job role, employer, institution, organisational affiliation, business address, business email address, business telephone number, professional biography, office location and related business contact details.

2.2 Communications and correspondence data

We may collect information contained in or generated through communications with ShoreVest, including emails, website contact forms, meeting requests, event registrations, calendar invitations, call logs, voicemail records, letters, messaging correspondence, notes of discussions, follow-up records, due diligence correspondence, relationship summaries and other records of business or investor interactions.

2.3 Investor-access, account and authentication data

Where you request access to restricted or investor-facing materials, we may collect account and access-related information such as usernames, login identifiers, encrypted passwords or password-reset records, access permissions, authentication credentials, multi-factor authentication details, session records, access logs, usage records, portal preferences, administrator actions and associated technical metadata.

2.4 Identity, authority and verification information

Where appropriate, we may collect information used to confirm your identity, your authority to act on behalf of an institution, your relationship to an organisation, and your eligibility to access certain information or engage in certain processes. This may include copies of identification documents where necessary, employer confirmations, role-based verification, signatures, title information, proof of authority or similar verification information.

2.5 Compliance and screening information

Where relevant to legal, regulatory, risk-management, investor-access, business-acceptance or onboarding processes, we may collect information for compliance and screening purposes. This may include sanctions screening data, politically exposed person checks, watchlist results, anti-money laundering and know-your-client information, source-of-funds-related data, tax residency information, legal entity relationship data, regulatory classification data and other information necessary to comply with applicable legal and regulatory obligations.

2.6 Technical, device and usage data

When you access or use the website, we may collect technical information and usage data, including IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, device identifiers, network information, language preferences, approximate geographic location derived from technical signals, referral source, pages viewed, files accessed or downloaded, time spent on pages, timestamps, navigation paths, session behaviour, error records, diagnostic information and similar website interaction data.

2.7 Cookie and similar technology data

We may collect information through cookies, pixels, tags, scripts, software development kits, local storage objects and other similar technologies, subject to applicable law. This may include session identifiers, settings, interaction data, aggregate website analytics, portal security data and related information used for website operation, functionality, analytics and security purposes.

2.8 Investor and diligence-related data

In connection with requests for access to investor materials, due diligence processes, relationship development or fund-related interactions, we may collect information about the institution you represent, your role in evaluating or authorising investments, your preferences, your diligence focus areas, investment profile, communications history, document access history, attendance at meetings or events, and similar investor-relations or diligence-related information.

2.9 Recruitment and career-related data

If you submit an application, CV or enquiry relating to employment or internships, we may collect your education history, employment history, professional qualifications, licences, language skills, compensation expectations, references, interview notes, right-to-work information, application materials and other information that you choose to provide or that is reasonably required for recruitment assessment and administration.

2.10 Sensitive personal information

ShoreVest does not generally seek to collect sensitive personal information through this website except where reasonably necessary and lawful in a specific context. In limited circumstances, such information may arise in connection with compliance obligations, identity verification, accessibility requirements, recruitment, dispute resolution, security incidents or similar matters. Where we process sensitive personal information, we will do so only to the extent permitted or required by applicable law and using additional safeguards where appropriate.

2.11 Information relating to representatives of organisations

In the institutional and business context in which ShoreVest operates, we may process personal information relating not only to direct individual contacts, but also to directors, officers, employees, representatives, trustees, consultants, beneficial owners, advisers, delegates, service providers or agents of investors, prospective investors, counterparties, portfolio-related entities, lenders, regulators and other business organisations.

We may use personal information to establish, protect, exercise or defend ShoreVest’s legal rights or legitimate interests, including in connection with disputes, investigations, complaints, regulatory enquiries, litigation, arbitration, enforcement of website terms or contractual rights, and preservation of evidence.

8. Data retention

ShoreVest retains personal information for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected or processed, including for business continuity, relationship management, website administration, access control, legal compliance, audit, evidentiary preservation, dispute resolution, fraud prevention, security monitoring and protection of rights.

Retention periods may vary depending on factors such as:

  • the nature and sensitivity of the information
  • the purpose for which the information was collected
  • whether there is an ongoing relationship with the relevant individual or institution
  • whether access to restricted materials has been requested or granted
  • whether legal, regulatory, tax, accounting or contractual obligations require continued retention
  • whether the information may be relevant to current or anticipated disputes, investigations or claims

When personal information is no longer required, ShoreVest will take reasonable steps to delete, anonymise, archive or securely isolate it in accordance with applicable law and internal retention standards.

9. Data security

ShoreVest maintains technical, administrative and organisational measures designed to protect personal information against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure, unauthorised access, misuse or other unlawful forms of processing.

Such measures may include, as appropriate:

  • access controls based on role and need-to-know
  • authentication measures and credential management
  • encryption or equivalent safeguards where appropriate
  • system monitoring and logging
  • network and endpoint security controls
  • vendor management and contractual safeguards
  • confidentiality measures and internal policies
  • information security training
  • incident response and escalation procedures
  • business continuity and resilience planning

Notwithstanding the foregoing, no website, portal, communication system, network, cloud environment, transmission channel or storage system can be guaranteed to be completely secure or immune from risk. ShoreVest therefore cannot and does not warrant absolute security.
