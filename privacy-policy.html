Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 3, 2026.

ShoreVest Partners, Ltd. and its affiliates (collectively, "ShoreVest," "we," "us," or "our") are committed to protecting personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and otherwise process personal information through our public website, investor portal, and related online or offline interactions that link to or reference this policy (collectively, the "Services").

1. Scope and Important Notices

  • This policy applies to information we process in connection with the Services.
  • This policy does not apply to third-party websites, services, or platforms linked from our Services; those parties maintain their own privacy practices.
  • Where local law requires additional notices (for example, specific U.S. state or EEA/UK disclosures), those notices are included in this policy.

2. Personal Information We Collect

a. Information you provide directly

  • Identifiers and contact details (for example: name, email address, mailing address, phone number, company, title).
  • Account and investor access data (for example: usernames, portal credentials, investor category, eligibility information).
  • Communications and submissions (for example: inquiries, subscription preferences, event registrations, files you submit, and correspondence content).
  • Professional and due diligence information you provide in connection with business relationships or investment discussions.

b. Information collected automatically

  • Device and browser data (for example: IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers, language, approximate location inferred from IP).
  • Usage data (for example: pages visited, links clicked, referring URLs, access times, interaction events, and session details).
  • Cookie and similar technology data as described in the Cookies and Similar Technologies section below.

c. Information from other sources

  • Service providers supporting analytics, security, communications, and website operations.
  • Public and commercial sources (for example: professional profiles, sanctions/AML checks where applicable, and corporate records).
  • Other parties you authorize to share information with us.

3. How We Use Personal Information

We may use personal information for the following purposes:

  • Provide, operate, secure, and improve the Services and user experience.
  • Respond to requests, manage relationships, and provide client or investor support.
  • Review investor access requests, eligibility, and related onboarding or due diligence workflows.
  • Send updates, insights, event notices, and other communications (subject to your communication preferences and applicable law).
  • Conduct analytics, diagnostics, auditing, and internal reporting.
  • Prevent fraud, misuse, and cybersecurity incidents; enforce policies and terms.
  • Comply with legal, regulatory, tax, accounting, and recordkeeping obligations.
  • Establish, exercise, or defend legal rights and manage disputes.

4. Legal Bases for Processing (EEA/UK/Similar Jurisdictions)

Where required by applicable law, we process personal information under one or more of the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract or steps requested prior to entering a contract.
  • Compliance with legal obligations.
  • Our legitimate interests (such as operating and securing our business and Services), provided those interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Your consent, where required (which you may withdraw at any time as permitted by law).

5. How We Disclose Personal Information

We do not sell personal information for money. We may disclose personal information to:

  • Affiliates and related entities for purposes consistent with this policy.
  • Service providers and vendors that perform functions on our behalf (for example: hosting, analytics, IT support, communications, data management, professional services).
  • Business partners for events, marketing, and collaboration activities where appropriate.
  • Regulators, law enforcement, courts, and governmental authorities when required by law, legal process, or to protect rights and safety.
  • Professional advisers (for example: legal, compliance, audit, and tax advisers).
  • Relevant parties in connection with an actual or proposed corporate transaction (for example: merger, financing, acquisition, reorganization, or asset transfer).

We may also disclose personal information where needed to investigate suspected fraud, unauthorized access, or other illegal activity, and to protect the rights, property, and safety of ShoreVest, our users, and others.

6. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We and our providers use cookies, pixels, local storage, and similar technologies to support site functionality, security, traffic measurement, and performance optimization.

  • Strictly necessary technologies: Required for website operation, security, and core functions.
  • Analytics technologies: Help us understand traffic and improve content and performance.
  • Preference technologies: Remember settings and improve user convenience.

You can control cookies through browser settings and, where available, cookie preference tools. Blocking some cookies may affect website functionality.

Where required by applicable law, we obtain consent before placing non-essential cookies and honor applicable browser-based opt-out preference signals (such as Global Privacy Control) for relevant processing.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, including to meet legal, regulatory, accounting, audit, dispute resolution, and enforcement requirements. Retention periods depend on data type, context, and applicable legal obligations.

8. Data Security

We apply administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect personal information. However, no security system is infallible, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. International Data Transfers

ShoreVest may process information in jurisdictions other than where you are located. Where required, we implement appropriate transfer safeguards under applicable law.

10. Your Privacy Rights and Choices

a. General rights (subject to law)

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to:

  • Request access to personal information we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Request deletion of personal information, subject to exceptions.
  • Request restriction of processing or object to certain processing activities.
  • Request portability of certain personal information.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

b. U.S. state privacy notices (including California)

  • Residents of certain U.S. states may have rights to know, access, correct, delete, and opt out of specific processing as defined by state law.
  • ShoreVest does not knowingly sell personal information for monetary consideration.
  • Where "sharing" for cross-context behavioral advertising is defined by law, you may request to opt out where applicable.
  • You may appoint an authorized agent to submit requests where permitted.
  • We will not unlawfully discriminate against you for exercising privacy rights.

How to submit a request: You may submit privacy rights requests by emailing privacy@shorevest.com. We may take reasonable steps to verify your identity (and, where relevant, your authorized-agent authority) before fulfilling your request, consistent with applicable law.

c. Marketing communications

You can opt out of promotional emails using the unsubscribe mechanism in those messages. Even after opting out, we may still send non-promotional administrative or relationship messages.

11. Do Not Track and Global Privacy Signals

Some browsers provide "Do Not Track" signals, and some jurisdictions recognize browser-based opt-out preference signals. We interpret and process such signals as required by applicable law.

12. Data Accuracy

We take reasonable steps to keep personal information accurate and current, and we encourage you to contact us if your information changes or you believe information we hold is inaccurate.

13. Anti-Fraud and Phishing Awareness

ShoreVest’s name and brand may be misused by third parties in phishing attempts or fraudulent communications. ShoreVest will not request your account password by email. If you receive suspicious communications claiming to be from ShoreVest, contact us using verified contact details.

14. Children’s Privacy

Our Services are intended for business and professional audiences and are not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under 16 through the Services.

15. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes become effective when posted with a revised effective date. Material updates will be communicated as required by applicable law.

16. Contact Us

For privacy questions, rights requests, or complaints, contact us at privacy@shorevest.com.

If you require our current mailing address for formal correspondence, please email privacy@shorevest.com and we will provide it.
