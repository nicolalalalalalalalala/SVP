# A look at the tools being deployed to help China’s banks dispose of their NPLs

**Series:** China Debt Dynamics  
**Volume / Issue:** Volume 3, Issue 5  
**Date:** October 16, 2019

---

China’s banks have spent the last few years ramping up the disposal of non-performing loans (NPLs), in part due to regulatory pressure and in part due to rising credit stress in the economy. As the volume of bad debt has increased, so too has the range of tools used to resolve it.

Historically, China relied on a relatively narrow set of mechanisms for dealing with NPLs, primarily through asset management companies (AMCs) established in the late 1990s to absorb bad loans from the banking system. These AMCs—often referred to as “bad banks”—played a central role in stabilizing the financial system following earlier periods of credit stress.

However, as the market has matured, the toolkit available for NPL disposal has expanded significantly. Today, Chinese banks and regulators employ a wide range of mechanisms to manage and resolve distressed assets. These include:

- Direct sales of NPL portfolios to AMCs and other qualified investors
- Judicial auctions of collateral through online platforms
- Debt-to-equity swaps (DES)
- Asset securitization of non-performing loans (NPL ABS)
- Loan restructurings and negotiated settlements

One of the most notable developments in recent years has been the rise of online judicial auction platforms. Courts increasingly use platforms such as Alibaba’s Taobao and JD.com to auction collateral associated with defaulted loans. These platforms have improved transparency and broadened participation, allowing a wider pool of buyers to access distressed assets.

Debt-to-equity swaps have also become an important tool, particularly for large state-owned enterprises. By converting debt into equity, banks can reduce leverage at troubled companies while retaining exposure to potential upside. However, these transactions require careful structuring and are often influenced by policy objectives.

Another key development is the growth of the NPL securitization market. Banks are increasingly packaging pools of distressed loans into asset-backed securities, which can then be sold to investors. This helps banks recycle capital and manage balance sheet risk, while providing investors with access to diversified portfolios of distressed assets.

Despite these innovations, challenges remain. Pricing transparency is still evolving, and the legal and operational complexities associated with NPL resolution can be significant. Additionally, the role of local governments and policy considerations can influence outcomes in ways that are not always predictable.

Nevertheless, the continued development of China’s NPL disposal infrastructure represents a significant step forward in the evolution of its financial system. For investors with the expertise to navigate these complexities, the market offers a growing set of opportunities.
