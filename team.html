Leadership and Investment

Responsible for sourcing, underwriting, execution and portfolio management across the platform.

Benjamin Fanger
Managing Partner & Founder

Ben Fanger has spent more than two decades building what is now the most experienced institutional private credit platform in China. He founded ShoreVest Partners in 2016, bringing with him the core senior team from Shoreline Capital — the firm he co-founded in 2004 and which he grew into one of the first institutionally scaled private credit managers in the Chinese market. Across both platforms, he has overseen the deployment of more than US$2 billion in Chinese private credit on behalf of sovereign wealth funds, pension funds, insurance companies, endowments, and family offices worldwide.

Fanger's engagement with Chinese distressed debt predates the market itself. While at the law firm O'Melveny & Myers, he worked on Morgan Stanley's 2001 acquisition of a large non-performing loan portfolio in China — the first successful large-scale foreign NPL purchase in the country's history. He subsequently earned his JD and MBA from the University of Chicago, closing his first Chinese NPL deals while still a student. Before turning to investment management full-time, he co-founded and sold two companies to public companies.

Over more than twenty years, Fanger has worked directly with Chinese banks, borrowers, courts, regulators, and government officials across thousands of distressed asset transactions. He has been fluent in spoken and written Mandarin for nearly thirty years. He is a licensed attorney in the State of California and sits on ShoreVest's Investment Committee.

Mecy Mei
Partner

Mecy Mei is a founding partner of ShoreVest and one of the most experienced practitioners in Chinese private credit investing. She served as a partner at Shoreline Capital for eight years before co-founding ShoreVest in 2016, giving her a continuous track record in this market stretching back to the period when institutional private credit in China was still being defined.

At ShoreVest, Mei co-leads ShoreVest Asset Solutions — the firm's proprietary debt servicing platform and a foundational competitive advantage in Chinese NPL investing. Her investment experience runs the full spectrum: sourcing, due diligence, deal structuring, acquisition and negotiation, post-investment monitoring, and exit. She has overseen the successful completion of numerous transactions across every phase of China's credit cycle. Earlier in her career she worked at Morningstar as a fund analyst, building debt and equity portfolios for institutional clients in China.

She was awarded the CFA designation in 2018. She holds a master's degree in economics from Nanjing University and a bachelor's degree in economics from Hefei University of Technology, and sits on ShoreVest's Investment Committee.

Hui Zheng
Partner

Hui Zheng brings to ShoreVest a combination of legal expertise and investment experience that is unusual even within China's specialist distressed credit market. She began her career as a practicing attorney at a Guangdong-based law firm, where she focused on the liquidation of non-performing loan assets — the precise legal terrain that ShoreVest navigates on every transaction. Her academic work in the field has been published, including the paper "Negative Impacts the New Bankruptcy Law has on the Liquidation of Non-performing Assets."

She joined the senior team at Shoreline Capital in 2008, served as a partner for eight years, and co-founded ShoreVest in 2016. At ShoreVest she co-leads ShoreVest Asset Solutions alongside Mecy Mei, and her investment mandate covers the complete credit lifecycle: sourcing, diligence, structuring, active management, and exit. Her legal formation provides a distinct analytical lens on counterparty risk, enforcement mechanics, and regulatory exposure across ShoreVest's portfolio.

She holds an LLM in economic law on foreign business from Sun Yat-sen University School of Law and a law degree from Nanchang University, and sits on ShoreVest's Investment Committee.

Ada Bi
Partner & CFO

Ada Bi has served as ShoreVest's Chief Financial Officer since the firm's founding in 2016, and as CFO of its predecessor firm Shoreline Capital from 2008. Across nearly two decades at the same investment platform, she has built and led the financial infrastructure through which more than US$2 billion in Chinese private credit has been deployed, managed, and resolved.

Her mandate spans the full operational lifecycle of ShoreVest's funds: financial reporting, treasury, tax, cash management, and the financial controls of invested projects. Bi holds the Fellow designation of the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (FCCA) and is a member of CPA Canada, holding the legacy Certified General Accountant designation. She holds a bachelor's degree in international trade from Guangdong University of Foreign Studies and sits on ShoreVest's Investment Committee.

Daoping Yang

John Jones
Director of Client Solutions (ex-Asia)

John Jones leads ShoreVest's client engagement across the Americas, the Middle East, and Europe. He joined the firm in April 2021, bringing more than fifteen years of experience across public and private credit, capital markets, and alternative investment strategy.

While pursuing his undergraduate degree, he spent two years on a Mandarin-language service mission.

He holds an MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and a BA in Economics from the University of Southern California.

Kelvin Chan

Dinny McMahon
Senior Advisory Partner

Dinny McMahon is one of the most authoritative English-language voices on China's financial system. As a Senior Advisory Partner, he works with ShoreVest's investment committee on China's macroeconomic environment, the state of its commercial banking system, non-performing loan volumes and trajectories, and the structural and regulatory factors that shape the firm's deal sourcing and execution.

McMahon spent a decade as a financial journalist in China — four years covering currency and foreign exchange markets for Dow Jones Newswires in Shanghai, followed by six years as Banking and Finance Correspondent for The Wall Street Journal in Beijing, where he tracked the accumulation of debt, the expansion of shadow banking, and the systemic fragilities building within China's state-directed financial system. He left The Wall Street Journal in 2015 to take up a fellowship at the Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars under the Kissinger Institute on China and the United States, where he wrote China's Great Wall of Debt: Shadow Banks, Ghost Cities, Massive Loans, and the End of the Chinese Miracle, published by Houghton Mifflin Harcourt in 2018. The Economist called it the closest any writer had come to covering the Chinese economy as Michael Lewis might. Reuters called it the best book on China's economic fragility.

He subsequently held a fellowship at MacroPolo, the Paulson Institute's think tank, researching China's financial system cleanup. He currently serves as Head of China Markets Research at Trivium China, contributes regularly to Foreign Affairs and The Wire China, and has testified before the U.S.-China Economic and Security Review Commission.

McMahon is fluent in Mandarin, having studied the language in Beijing and Kunming before spending a year studying international relations in Chinese at the Hopkins-Nanjing Center, the Johns Hopkins SAIS campus in Nanjing. He holds a double degree in economics and Chinese from the University of New South Wales.
