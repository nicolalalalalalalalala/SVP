The Firm

ShoreVest's asset-backed private credit strategy traces back to 2004. The current ShoreVest platform was established in 2016 by the senior team that had previously executed the same strategy through predecessor vehicles.

Today the firm focuses on three core sub-strategies in China: asset-based lending, asset restructuring, and debt resolutions, with headquarters in Guangzhou and a satellite office in Shanghai, and the investment team based locally in China.

ShoreVest is designed for a market where legal structure, local execution, and recoverability matter as much as headline yield — using directly enforceable onshore positions, conservative 30–60% LTV/CTV entry points, and control from underwriting through resolution.

Why this market requires specialisation.

China private credit cannot be evaluated as a generic extension of global direct lending. Local legal architecture and enforcement dynamics shape realized outcomes.

Documentation and security are primary risk controls

Return is often determined by lien ranking, enforceability, and documentary precision.

Enforcement is process-intensive

Recovery requires active execution through court process, restructuring, and negotiation.

Origination is relationship-driven

Opportunities emerge via local counterparties before broad syndication.

Borrower and sponsor behavior varies by situation

Pattern recognition under pressure is central to managing outcomes.

Servicing capability affects value retention

Monitoring and intervention after close shape loss severity and options.

Global experience alone is insufficient

Without local execution capacity, investors can hold paper without controlling outcomes.
