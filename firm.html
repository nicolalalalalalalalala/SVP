The Firm

ShoreVest's China private credit strategy traces back to 2004. The current platform was established in 2016 by the senior team that had built and operated predecessor vehicles across collateral-backed lending, asset restructuring, and NPL resolution.

The firm focuses exclusively on three sub-strategies in China: asset-based lending, asset restructuring, and debt resolutions. Headquarters and core execution teams are based in Guangzhou, with onshore coverage across sourcing, underwriting, servicing, and recovery.

ShoreVest is built for markets where legal structure, local execution, and recoverability matter as much as yield. The platform emphasises enforceable onshore claims, conservative entry basis, and active control from origination through exit.

How the platform operates.

Three operating pillars govern origination, underwriting, and portfolio management from entry through exit.

Proprietary Sourcing

Direct relationships with banks, AMCs, law firms, and servicers built over two decades. Opportunities are accessed before competitive auction through bilateral sourcing or selective portfolio acquisition — not through intermediated processes.

Disciplined Underwriting

Every transaction is anchored to hard-asset collateral with conservative loan-to-value or cost-to-value entry points of typically 30–60%, and first-lien or otherwise directly enforceable positioning under PRC law. Structure and covenants are designed before return targets. Downside is defined before upside. Each deal is underwritten from downside-out — enforceability, collateral quality, and conservative entry basis are established first. Return follows from structure, not from assumptions about exit conditions.

Alignment of Interests

Senior team continuity of approximately twenty years. GP commitment of US$5 million or 1% of fund commitments. The individuals who approve investments are accountable for monitoring and exit — there is no separation between the decision-makers and the people responsible for outcomes.

Proprietary sourcing in practice

Relationship density across banks, AMCs, law firms, and servicers helps ShoreVest screen opportunities before broad auctions, preserving pricing and structure flexibility.

Where we operate.

Headquarters in Guangzhou. Satellite office in Shanghai. The investment team is based in China — sourcing, diligence, structuring, servicing, and resolution run through an integrated onshore process. Local presence is not a differentiator. It is a prerequisite.

Why China requires a different approach.

China private credit is not a plug-and-play extension of global direct lending. Local legal architecture, court process, and workout execution materially shape realised outcomes. Each of the following represents a structural feature of the market — not a risk to be disclosed, but a condition to be managed.

DOCUMENTATION AND LEGAL STRUCTURE ARE PRIMARY RISK CONTROLS

Return is often determined by lien ranking, enforceability, and documentary precision — not by credit spreads or borrower covenants alone.

ENFORCEMENT IS PROCESS-INTENSIVE

Recovery requires active execution through PRC court process, creditor coordination, and negotiated resolution. It cannot be outsourced to a servicer unfamiliar with local practice.

ORIGINATION IS RELATIONSHIP-DRIVEN

Deal flow reaches experienced local managers before broad syndication. Access is built over years — it cannot be acquired through a placement agent.

BORROWER BEHAVIOUR VARIES BY SITUATION

Pattern recognition under pressure is central to managing outcomes. Sponsors and borrowers respond differently across market cycles. Experience across multiple cycles is the only reliable filter.

SERVICING CAPABILITY DETERMINES LOSS SEVERITY

Active monitoring and timely intervention after close shape recovery outcomes. Passive surveillance is insufficient in a market where conditions can deteriorate quickly.

GLOBAL CREDIT EXPERIENCE ALONE IS INSUFFICIENT

Without onshore legal capacity, local relationships, and PRC enforcement experience, investors can hold a paper claim without the ability to realise its value.

How execution is concentrated.

Compared with a typical offshore fund model, ShoreVest retains sourcing, legal structuring, investment committee governance, and recovery coordination in-house. That concentration reflects the structural reality of China's credit market: enforceability and workout execution determine realised value, and those workstreams cannot be effectively outsourced.
