The Firm

ShoreVest is a specialist private credit platform focused on asset-backed investments in China. Since 2004, the firm has built its approach around enforceable collateral, conservative entry levels, and active control through sourcing, underwriting, servicing, and recovery.

Built for control in complex credit

ShoreVest focuses on three areas of China private credit: asset-based lending, asset restructurings, and debt resolutions. Across each, the common discipline is the same: directly enforceable claims, hard-asset collateral, and conservative entry points designed to protect principal before upside is pursued.

Asset-based lending

Senior secured financing against identifiable hard-asset collateral with documented enforcement pathways.

Asset restructurings

Recapitalisation and restructuring situations where control rights and collateral priority can be contractually preserved.

Debt resolutions

Distressed and non-performing credit situations requiring coordinated legal process, servicing control, and recovery execution.

Why this model works in China

In China private credit, outcomes are determined by enforceable onshore rights, collateral quality, and local execution discipline - not document form alone.

  • Practical control often depends on local legal standing and enforceable onshore rights, especially when borrower conditions deteriorate.

  • Recovery outcomes can vary materially based on process execution, court timelines, and regional enforcement behavior.

  • Underwriting is grounded in executable disposal routes and realistic timing assumptions rather than mark-to-model expectations.

  • ShoreVest prioritizes structures with direct claim enforceability and avoids exposures where legal remedies are uncertain.

  • The focus remains on hard collateral and recoverability, limiting dependence on uncertain project completion cycles.

  • Alignment at entry helps preserve downside protection and creates clearer execution pathways through monitoring and resolution.
