The Firm

ShoreVest's asset-backed private credit strategy traces back to 2004. The current ShoreVest platform was established in 2016 by the senior team that had previously executed the same strategy through predecessor vehicles.

Today the firm focuses on three core sub-strategies in China: asset-based lending, asset restructuring, and debt resolutions, with headquarters in Guangzhou and a satellite office in Shanghai, and the investment team based locally in China.

ShoreVest is designed for a market where legal structure, local execution, and recoverability matter as much as headline yield — using directly enforceable onshore positions, conservative 30–60% LTV/CTV entry points, and control from underwriting through resolution.

Why this market requires specialisation.

China private credit cannot be evaluated as a generic extension of global direct lending. Local legal architecture, enforcement practice, information asymmetry, and restructuring dynamics all shape realised outcomes.

Documentation and security are primary risk controls

In contested situations, return is often determined by lien ranking, collateral enforceability, and documentary precision. Underwriting therefore extends into legal structuring, not only credit modeling.

Enforcement is process-intensive

Recovery is rarely a passive legal event. It requires persistent execution through court process, negotiation, restructuring pathways, and asset-level follow-through.

Origination is relationship-driven

Many opportunities emerge through local counterparties, banks, and advisors before broad market syndication. Access and judgment must be built on the ground over time.

Borrower and sponsor behaviour varies by situation

Capital structure dynamics and willingness to engage can shift quickly under pressure. Local pattern recognition matters in setting terms, covenants, and engagement strategy.

Servicing capability affects value retention

Monitoring, covenant management, and active intervention after close shape both loss severity and resolution options when conditions deteriorate.

Global experience alone is insufficient

Cross-border credit perspective is useful, but without integrated local execution capacity, investors can hold paper without controlling outcomes.

How the platform is built.

The firm is owned and led by senior investment professionals, with leadership accountability tied directly to investment outcomes. ShoreVest, its onshore entities, and ShoreVest Asset Solutions operate as an integrated credit execution platform.
