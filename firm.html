The Firm

ShoreVest's China private credit strategy traces back to 2004. The current ShoreVest platform was established in 2016 by the senior team that had previously executed related collateral-backed and restructuring strategies through predecessor vehicles.

Today the firm focuses on three core sub-strategies in China: asset-based lending, asset restructuring, and debt resolutions. Headquarters and core execution teams are based in Guangzhou, with onshore coverage across sourcing, underwriting, and recovery.

ShoreVest is built for markets where legal structure, local execution, and recoverability matter as much as coupon. The platform emphasizes enforceable onshore claims, conservative entry basis, and active control from underwriting through resolution and exit.

Why this market requires specialization.

China private credit is not a plug-and-play extension of global direct lending. Local legal architecture, court process, and workout execution materially shape realized outcomes.

Documentation and security are primary risk controls

Return is often determined by lien ranking, enforceability, and documentary precision.

Enforcement is process-intensive

Recovery requires active execution through court process, restructuring, and negotiation.

Origination is relationship-driven

Opportunities emerge via local counterparties before broad syndication.

Borrower and sponsor behavior varies by situation

Pattern recognition under pressure is central to managing outcomes.

Servicing capability affects value retention

Monitoring and intervention after close shape loss severity and options.

Global experience alone is insufficient

Without local execution capacity, investors can hold paper without controlling outcomes.

Why the capability map matters

Compared with a typical offshore fund model, ShoreVest retains more of the execution-critical workstreams in-house through underwriting and recovery. That concentration reflects the structural reality of China's credit market, where enforceability and workout execution determine realized value.

Decision protocol

The committee meets weekly. Investment decisions require the Managing Partner's vote and a 3/4 super-majority. The individuals who approve entry are accountable for monitoring and exit.

All four committee members participate in screening and diligence before a vote, so legal, underwriting, and execution issues are surfaced before approval.
