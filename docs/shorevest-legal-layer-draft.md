# ShoreVest Website Legal & Disclaimer Layer (Draft)

## 1) Recommended legal page stack

### Required pages and one-line purpose

1. **Privacy Policy** — Explains what personal data ShoreVest collects, why, how it is used, shared, retained, and what rights may apply.
2. **Terms of Use** — Sets the legal terms for using the website, including permitted use, liability limits, and governing law.
3. **Important Disclosures / Legal Notice** — Core no-offer, no-solicitation, no-advice, no-reliance, accuracy, forward-looking, and performance disclaimers.
4. **Cookie Notice** — Explains cookies/trackers used on the site and user choices/consent controls by category.
5. **Investor Access Terms** — Terms governing entry to restricted/investor materials, including qualification and jurisdiction representations.
6. **Restricted Materials Notice** — Front-door legend explaining that certain content is confidential, restricted, and not for public circulation.
7. **Contact Form Privacy Notice** — Short just-in-time privacy statement specific to inquiry forms and submissions.
8. **Download Disclaimer** — Disclaimer shown before and with downloadable materials, especially where content may be market commentary.
9. **Insights / Commentary Disclaimer** — Clarifies that publications are informational, non-advisory, non-recommendation commentary.
10. **Copyright / Intellectual Property Notice** — States ownership and permitted/forbidden reuse of site content, brand, and publications.

### What can be merged vs remain separate

**Can be merged (recommended):**
- **Important Disclosures / Legal Notice + Restricted Materials Notice + Download Disclaimer + Insights / Commentary Disclaimer** into one “Important Disclosures” page with anchored sections.
- **Contact Form Privacy Notice** can exist as a section inside Privacy Policy **and** still appear as short just-in-time form notice.
- **Copyright / Intellectual Property Notice** can be a section within Terms of Use if you want a tighter legal stack.

**Should remain separate (recommended):**
- **Privacy Policy** (standalone).
- **Terms of Use** (standalone).
- **Cookie Notice** (standalone, because consent tooling should map cleanly).
- **Investor Access Terms** (standalone, because this is high-friction gating language and should be independently versioned).

## 8) Replace these phrases before launch

1. **“Market leader”**  
   Risk: unverifiable superiority claim.  
   Safer: “Active participant in China private credit.”

2. **“Best-in-class”**  
   Risk: promotional superlative; hard to substantiate.  
   Safer: “Institutional-focused approach.”

3. **“Predictable exits”**  
   Risk: implies certainty of outcomes.  
   Safer: “Exit outcomes depend on market and case-specific factors.”

4. **“Superior returns”**  
   Risk: performance promise/comparative claim.  
   Safer: “Risk-adjusted return objective.”

5. **“Never lost money”**  
   Risk: absolute performance claim, high enforcement risk.  
   Safer: “Past results vary across periods and investments.”

6. **“Low risk”**  
   Risk: understates material risks.  
   Safer: “Risk-managed approach with material downside scenarios.”

7. **“Safe”**  
   Risk: implies capital protection.  
   Safer: “Subject to investment risk, including loss of capital.”

8. **“Proven”**  
   Risk: can imply guaranteed repeatability.  
   Safer: “Established experience in the strategy.”

9. **“Unique”**  
   Risk: absolute differentiation claim.  
   Safer: “Differentiated in selected respects.”

10. **“Unmatched”**  
    Risk: unverifiable superlative.  
    Safer: “Competitive within its focus area.”

11. **“Institutional-grade”**  
    Risk: can imply regulated standard/certification.  
    Safer: “Designed for institutional audiences.”

12. **“Invest with us”**  
    Risk: direct solicitation language.  
    Safer: “Contact us regarding eligibility and available information.”

13. **“Partner with us”**  
    Risk: can imply invitation to invest.  
    Safer: “Discuss strategic alignment and eligibility.”

14. **“Access opportunities”**  
    Risk: promotional and potentially solicitative.  
    Safer: “Review market commentary and strategy information.”

15. **“Top quartile”**  
    Risk: ranking claim requiring robust, current substantiation.  
    Safer: “Performance information available to eligible recipients with context.”

16. **“Enforcement certainty”**  
    Risk: legal outcome guarantee.  
    Safer: “Enforcement outcomes depend on legal and factual circumstances.”

17. **“Secure returns”**  
    Risk: promise of returns/capital protection.  
    Safer: “Return objectives are subject to risk and uncertainty.”

18. **“Resilient performance”**  
    Risk: broad implied performance assurance.  
    Safer: “Performance has varied across market environments.”

19. **“Downside protected”**  
    Risk: guarantee-like downside claim.  
    Safer: “Downside mitigation measures may reduce but not eliminate loss risk.”

20. **“Leading platform”**  
    Risk: comparative superiority claim.  
    Safer: “Established platform in its target segment.”

21. **“Consistent alpha”**  
    Risk: implied persistent outperformance promise.  
    Safer: “Seeks attractive risk-adjusted outcomes over time.”

22. **“High-conviction winners”**  
    Risk: selective, promotional framing.  
    Safer: “Select opportunities subject to underwriting criteria.”

23. **“Defensive by design”**  
    Risk: may imply low-loss assurance.  
    Safer: “Structured with risk controls; losses remain possible.”

24. **“Reliable income”**  
    Risk: implies stable distribution/return certainty.  
    Safer: “Income outcomes may vary and are not guaranteed.”

7. **Privacy rights language** aligns to all applicable laws for expected traffic (including cross-border users).
8. **Cookie notice and consent flows** match actual tracker deployment and regional consent requirements.
9. **Data retention and transfer statements** match operational practice and vendor contracts.
10. **Contact/newsletter disclosures** match actual processing purposes and lawful bases.
11. **Restricted-material gating logs** (acknowledgement text versions, timestamps, IP, jurisdiction fields) are adequate for evidentiary use.

13. **Governing law/venue clauses** are enforceable and consistent with entity structure.
14. **External-link and third-party data disclaimers** are adequate for referenced sources.
15. **Document footer legends** are present in every restricted PDF and version-controlled.
