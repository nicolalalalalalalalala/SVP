# ShoreVest Website Refinement Prompt (Codex)

You are updating the existing ShoreVest website and related external-facing materials.

This is **not** a rebuild. Do not redesign the site from scratch. Do not replace the current website with a new concept. Do not create a new information architecture unless a very minor structural adjustment is clearly necessary.

Work within the existing website and apply the ShoreVest brand system, copy direction, and visual discipline to what is already there.

## Primary instruction

Apply the refined ShoreVest brand and messaging system to the current website. This is an apply-to-existing-site brief, not a rebuild brief.

## Core objective

Make the existing website feel sharper, more coherent, more credible, and more institutionally controlled, while preserving the current structure as much as possible. Improve copy, hierarchy, spacing, imagery, and styling selectively. Keep what already works. Refine what does not.

## Non-negotiable constraints

- Do not rebuild the website from scratch.
- Do not invent a new sitemap.
- Do not add unsupported facts.
- Do not introduce new claims beyond approved ShoreVest materials.
- Do not use stock photos.
- Do not substitute fonts.
- Do not substitute colours.
- Do not create a visual system outside the uploaded ShoreVest brand materials.
- Do not over-poeticise the copy.
- Do not turn the site into a generic private-capital website.

## Brand application rules (mandatory)

Apply the uploaded ShoreVest colour, typography, and logo system exactly.

### 1) Xuan Paper (primary background family)

- Xuan Paper Light: `#FAF6EE`
- Xuan Paper: `#F2ECD8`
- Xuan Paper Dark: `#D8CEB0`

Use this instead of cold white. Main page backgrounds should sit in the Xuan Paper family unless there is a specific reason not to. White should not dominate the site. Xuan Paper is the default surface language.

### 2) Cinnabar (primary brand red)

- Cinnabar Light: `#E8998F`
- Cinnabar: `#C93B2A`
- Cinnabar Dark: `#8A1F12`

Use Cinnabar for key emphasis, thin rules, selected active states, highlights, and the Chinese wordmark treatment where appropriate. Do not use random other reds. Do not shift the hue toward burgundy or generic corporate red.

### 3) Blue-Green (water/accent family)

- Blue-Green Light: `#7AAFAF`
- Blue-Green: `#3D7070`
- Blue-Green Dark: `#1E4040`

Use this as the shore/water accent family for restrained highlights, secondary accents, and subtle visual support. This should help bring “shore” and water into the brand world without turning the site bright or decorative.

### 4) Ink Green (foliage/depth family)

- Ink Green Light: `#4A7A40`
- Ink Green: `#2E4A18`
- Ink Green Dark: `#0F2206`

Use sparingly for depth, foliage cues, and supporting accents. Do not let the site become green-led. This is a secondary supporting family, not the primary brand colour.

## Typography (mandatory)

**English text:** DIN 2014 only.

**Chinese text:** Noto Serif SC only.

- Do not substitute either typeface.
- Do not use fallback system fonts in any intentional design application.
- Do not use condensed substitutes.
- Do not use sans-serif for Chinese.
- Do not mix simplified and traditional Chinese in the same layout.
- SHOREVEST wordmark tracking should remain `+5`.
- English and Chinese lockups must have equal visual weight.
- Where bilingual lockups are used, separate them with a thin cinnabar rule.

## Logo rules (mandatory)

Use only approved ShoreVest logo variants from the uploaded logo guide.

Approved variants include:

- full colour
- full colour on black
- cinnabar
- cinnabar on black
- blue-green
- ink green
- black and off-white
- off-white on black

Do not create new logo colourways. Do not distort the logo. Do not alter spacing. Do not apply effects, shadows, gradients, or decorative containers unless already approved.

## Visual direction

Balance the visual world between:

- endurance, patience, permanence (the redwood/tree logic)
- shore, water, edge, movement, coastal positioning

Apply this subtly through:

- restrained imagery
- colour accents
- texture
- section backgrounds
- language cues

Do not use beach, resort, turquoise, lifestyle, or obvious stock water imagery. Think dark water, shoreline, estuary, harbour, mist, tidal edge, reflection, quiet movement.

Do not overdo tree imagery either. The site should not look like a forestry brand.

## Copy and structure direction

Do not rewrite the site wholesale. Refine the current pages.

The website should get into the actual story faster:

1. Story
2. Proof
3. Stats

Reduce generic throat-clearing. Explain earlier why ShoreVest made sense in China, then move into proof points, then statistics.

Keep storytelling subtle and factual. Do not write vague “bridge between cultures” copy. A restrained version is acceptable only if grounded in approved materials (for example, international discipline and local China execution), but keep it controlled.

## Page-by-page instruction

### Homepage

Do not rebuild it. Refine the existing hero, supporting copy, and visual treatment so the page explains ShoreVest’s reason for existing in China faster and more clearly. Apply the correct colour and typography system.

### Firm page

Do not redesign from scratch. Improve the existing page so the operating model, local execution logic, and institutional structure are clearer. Reduce awkward oversized text and any generic or slogan-heavy language.

### Strategy page

Keep the current page structure. Tighten wording, remove overclaiming, and apply cleaner hierarchy and brand styling.

### Team page

Refine the existing layout and bios presentation. Keep it controlled. LinkedIn links may be added selectively and quietly if useful, but do not let the page become social-media led.

### Press and insights

Improve the feel and usefulness of the existing pages. Keep them aligned to the analytical ShoreVest tone seen in China Debt Dynamics. Do not create empty promotional shells.

## Implementation method

For each existing page or section:

- keep what is already strong
- revise weak or generic copy
- correct colours to brand-approved colours
- correct fonts to brand-approved fonts
- correct logo usage to approved variants
- improve spacing and hierarchy where needed
- do not rebuild the page unnecessarily
- remove unsupported claims

## Output expectation

Provide targeted edits to the existing site, not a replacement concept.

For each page or section, state:

- what to keep
- what to revise
- what to remove
- what colour/font/logo corrections to make
- what visual refinements to apply

If any current page element conflicts with the uploaded brand guide, the uploaded brand guide takes precedence.
