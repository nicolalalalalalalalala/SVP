ASSET-BACKED PRIVATE CREDIT IN CHINA.

ShoreVest invests in asset-backed private credit situations across China — structured around enforceable collateral, conservative entry, and recovery led by the firm’s onshore team.

Operating in China since 2004 · First-lien downside protection · Onshore execution.

MARKET CONTEXT

WHY CHINA, WHY NOW.

China is the world’s second-largest credit market, yet remains materially under-allocated in most global portfolios. That underexposure is itself an active risk decision — and one that becomes harder to justify as China’s debt capital markets continue to develop.

ShoreVest’s strategy focuses on situations where complexity reduces competition and widens spread for enforceable secured claims. Outcomes are determined by structure, legal priority, and execution — not by macro narrative.

STRUCTURAL SPREAD

Complexity reduces competition. Enforceable secured claims can earn wider spread than the underlying credit risk would suggest.

ENTRY DISCIPLINE

Conservative loan-to-value and cost-to-value bands provide a meaningful margin of safety regardless of vintage.

OPERATING RECORD

ShoreVest has underwritten through multiple NPL and policy cycles since 2004, with the same first-lien discipline.

CAPITAL ROTATION FRAMEWORK

HOW DEPLOYMENT SHIFTS WITH OPPORTUNITY.

Allocation across the three sub-strategies shifts with market conditions, liquidity, and the relative attractiveness of new origination versus resolution. Every sleeve is screened through the same legal-control and recovery-first underwriting standard regardless of weight.

Across most market regimes, allocation is balanced across all three sub-strategies based on bottom-up opportunity rather than top-down rotation.

When new-origination spreads compress, capital weights toward asset restructuring and debt resolution situations where complexity creates opportunity.

When dislocation is elevated, capital weights toward new origination with strong legal control while retaining capacity for resolution work.

Allocations shift with opportunity. Underwriting does not.

RECOVERY ARCHITECTURE

WHERE RECOVERY ACTUALLY HAPPENS.

Most LPs assume Chinese courts and auction systems do not work for foreign creditors. ShoreVest’s twenty-year track record says they do — when claim packages are built correctly. Outcomes are driven by documentation quality, venue selection, and the ability to navigate enforcement infrastructure that few non-onshore platforms can access.

COURT THROUGHPUT

Secured collateral cases progress on a defined cadence in the right venues. Building the claim package correctly — collateral perfection, documentation chain, jurisdiction selection — determines timeline as much as the underlying credit.

AUCTION INFRASTRUCTURE

Public auction rails support price discovery and monetization across a broad set of collateral types, from real estate to operating assets to receivables.

EXIT OPTIONALITY

Negotiated settlement, secondary sale, and court-led realization create multiple pathways to monetization. Rarely is recovery dependent on a single route.

RISK FRAMEWORK

RISK CONTROLS.

Downside controls are embedded in both deal-level underwriting and portfolio construction.

Underwriting Controls

  • Conservative LTV/CTV bands with stressed recovery assumptions.
  • First-lien claims and enforceability review under PRC-law documentation.
  • No reliance on refinancing to realize value.

Portfolio Controls

  • Diversification by borrower, collateral, province, and vintage.
  • Continuous surveillance from origination through disposition.
  • Predefined exit pathways to reduce duration and execution risk.

China onshore secured credit is driven by collateral recovery and legal process more than by public spread or equity beta. The strategy has historically shown lower correlation to broad benchmarks — though correlation is not protection against loss.
