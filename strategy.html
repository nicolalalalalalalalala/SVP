Strategy

Strategy

Asset-backed private credit in China, structured around enforceability, collateral coverage, and defined recovery pathways.

Page navigation

These navigation links are provided for reading flow and do not imply section priority.

Opening frame

A recoverability-driven private credit strategy.

ShoreVest focuses on asset-backed private credit in China, with an emphasis on enforceability, collateral coverage and defined recovery pathways.

The strategy is designed for situations in which outcomes are determined primarily by capital structure, legal positioning and control, rather than by market direction alone.

What we invest in

ShoreVest invests across three principal segments, each defined by clear underwriting conditions at entry.

Asset-based lending

Senior secured financings against identifiable collateral where borrowers need flexible capital not available through traditional channels. ShoreVest invests only where seniority, collateral visibility and a defined enforcement pathway are established at entry.

Asset restructurings

Situations where viable assets sit within impaired or over-levered capital structures. ShoreVest invests where structure can be reset to improve creditor position and where a credible route to resolution control is available from the outset.

Debt resolutions

Acquisition and resolution of distressed or non-performing assets. ShoreVest invests where entry is below recoverable value and legal or consensual recovery processes can be executed with defined jurisdictional pathways.

Why this opportunity exists

China’s credit markets are large, locally driven and structurally uneven in how capital is allocated and enforced.

Credit outcomes are shaped by legal enforceability, creditor positioning, asset-level control and local execution.

In many situations, the gap between offshore claims and onshore assets materially affects recovery outcomes.

What we avoid

The strategy is defined as much by exclusion as by inclusion.

ShoreVest generally avoids

  • Unsecured exposure or structures with weak collateral perfection.
  • Structurally subordinated positions without enforceable senior recourse.
  • Speculative development risk without contracted cash-flow support.

And excludes situations with

  • Repayment dependent primarily on refinancing or market recovery rather than enforceable value.
  • Assets without a credible and time-bounded path to realization.
  • Legal structures where creditor remedies or jurisdiction are unclear.

This discipline is central to downside protection.

How returns are generated

Return is generated through four mechanisms:

  • Contractual yield: coupon income from secured positions with defined covenants and protections.
  • Entry discount: acquisition below assessed recoverable value to embed a margin at entry.
  • Structural positioning: legal and capital-structure seniority designed to improve recovery outcomes.
  • Resolution control: active influence over timeline and execution of legal or consensual workouts.

Return is not expected to be dependent on asset appreciation.

Downside control

Downside protection is the organizing principle of the strategy.

This is implemented through

  • Collateral: exposure supported by identifiable assets with an assessed recovery basis.
  • Legal priority: positions structured for enforceable seniority in relevant jurisdictions.
  • Control: investments selected where ShoreVest can influence outcome through structure or process.

Execution discipline

  • Entry discipline: capital deployed at levels with a margin relative to recoverable value.
  • Execution: recovery pathways assessed at underwriting and actively managed through resolution.

Over a 20-year operating history spanning more than 50 private credit transactions, the core team reports no realised principal loss at the individual deal level.

Past performance is not indicative of future results. All investments involve risk including possible loss of principal.

Deployment environment

Current market conditions include refinancing pressure, asset disposals and creditor repositioning across multiple sectors.

The firm believes these conditions can support deployment in structured lending, transitional capital and resolution-driven credit, subject to selective underwriting and clear recovery pathways.

Summary

The strategy is based on entering below recoverable value, structuring for control and executing through legal and operational processes.
