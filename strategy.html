Our Strategy

ShoreVest deploys asset-backed private credit into enforceable structures, conservative entry basis, and recovery-led exits in China's onshore market.

Market Context

Market dislocation is a structural entry condition.

China remains the world's second-largest credit market, yet many global portfolios remain materially under-allocated for non-fundamental reasons. That underexposure is itself an active risk decision.

ShoreVest's strategy focuses on where spread and collateral coverage improve when complexity reduces competition. Structure, legal priority, and execution cadence determine outcomes more than macro narrative.

  • First-lien secured onshore claims have materially different recovery dynamics from offshore unsecured exposure.
  • Vintage windows with weaker sentiment have historically supported better pricing and stronger downside buffers.
  • ShoreVest has operated continuously since 2004 across multiple NPL and policy cycles with structure-first underwriting.

Asset Restructuring & Workouts

Debt Resolutions & Recoveries

Risk Controls and Downside Governance

Downside management is structural, not discretionary. Portfolio construction is designed to protect principal first and monetize upside through execution.

Underwriting Controls

  • Conservative entry LTV/CTV bands with stress-tested recovery assumptions.
  • Priority claims, enforceability review, and PRC-law documentation standards.
  • No dependence on refinancing to complete value realization.

Portfolio Controls

  • Diversification by borrower, collateral, vintage, and province.
  • Continuous surveillance from origination through disposition.
  • Multiple predefined exits reduce duration and execution risk.
