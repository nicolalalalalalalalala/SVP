Strategy

Strategy

Asset-backed private credit in China, structured around enforceability, collateral coverage, and defined recovery pathways.

What we invest in

ShoreVest invests across three segments, each selected only when underwriting conditions are explicit at entry.

Asset-based lending

Senior secured financings against identifiable collateral when flexible capital is needed and seniority, collateral visibility, and enforceability are explicit at entry.

Asset restructurings

Situations with viable assets inside impaired capital structures, where terms can be reset to improve creditor position and a clear route to resolution control exists.

Debt resolutions

Acquisition and resolution of distressed or non-performing assets where entry is below recoverable value and legal or consensual recovery can be executed through defined pathways.

Goal: stay where legal priority, collateral and execution control can materially improve recovery outcomes.

Why this opportunity exists

China’s credit markets are large, locally driven and uneven in how capital is allocated and enforced. Credit outcomes are shaped by legal enforceability, creditor positioning, asset-level control and local execution. In many situations, the gap between offshore claims and onshore assets materially affects recovery outcomes.

What we avoid

The strategy is defined as much by exclusion as by inclusion.

  • Unsecured exposure or weak collateral perfection.
  • Structural subordination without enforceable senior recourse.
  • Speculative development risk without contracted cash-flow support.
  • Repayment reliant on refinancing or market recovery.
  • Assets without a credible, time-bounded realization path.
  • Legal structures with unclear remedies or jurisdiction.

This discipline is central to downside protection.

How returns are generated

Return is generated through four mechanisms:

  • Contractual yield: coupon income from secured positions with defined covenants and protections.
  • Entry discount: acquisition below assessed recoverable value to embed a margin at entry.
  • Structural positioning: legal and capital-structure seniority designed to improve recovery outcomes.
  • Resolution control: active influence over timeline and execution of legal or consensual debt resolutions.

Return is not expected to be dependent on asset appreciation.

Downside control

Downside protection is the organizing principle of the strategy.

Collateral

Exposure supported by identifiable assets with assessed recovery basis.

Legal priority

Positions structured for enforceable seniority in relevant jurisdictions.

Control

Investments selected where outcomes can be influenced through structure or process.

Entry discipline

Capital deployed only where entry carries margin to recoverable value.

Execution

Recovery pathways defined at underwriting and actively managed through resolution.

Over a 20-year operating history since 2004, spanning more than 50 private credit transactions, the core team reports no realised principal loss at the individual deal level.

Past performance is not indicative of future results. All investments involve risk including possible loss of principal.

Deployment environment

Current market conditions include refinancing pressure, asset disposals and creditor repositioning across multiple sectors.

This can support selective deployment in structured lending, transitional capital and resolution-driven credit.

Summary

ShoreVest seeks consistent return through entry discipline, legal priority and active resolution execution.
