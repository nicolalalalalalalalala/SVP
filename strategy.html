MARKET CONTEXT

MARKET DISLOCATION IS A STRUCTURAL ENTRY CONDITION.

China remains the world's second-largest credit market, yet many global portfolios remain materially under-allocated for non-fundamental reasons. That underexposure is itself an active risk decision.

ShoreVest focuses on situations where spread and collateral coverage improve because complexity reduces competition. Structure, legal priority, and execution cadence determine outcomes more than macro narrative.

STRUCTURAL SPREAD Complexity can reduce competition and widen spread for enforceable secured claims.
VINTAGE EFFECT Periods of weaker sentiment have historically supported better entry basis and downside buffers.
OPERATING RECORD ShoreVest has underwritten through multiple NPL and policy cycles since 2004.
THREE SUB-STRATEGIES

ONE CREDIT STANDARD, THREE ROUTES TO RECOVERY.

Deployment shifts with market conditions, but the underwriting standard does not: collateral priority, enforceability, and recovery planning are required before capital is committed.

01

ASSET-BASED LENDING

First-lien, asset-backed financing to well-performing companies within China's private credit market.

02

Credit clean-up situations involving good underlying assets and challenged balance sheets, including select distressed debt.

03

DEBT RESOLUTIONS

Working with financial institutions seeking to divest distressed portfolios, including non-performing loans.

UNDERWRITING STANDARD

COLLATERAL FIRST. LEGAL PRIORITY SECOND. YIELD LAST.

ShoreVest underwrites from recoverable value backward. Each investment is tested against collateral quality, claim priority, enforceability, exit pathway, and duration risk before return potential is considered.

COLLATERAL CONTROL

Entry basis is assessed against stressed recovery value, not optimistic refinancing assumptions.

LEGAL ENFORCEABILITY

Documentation and venue strategy are reviewed before capital is committed.

RECOVERY PLANNING

Exit routes are mapped upfront, including negotiated, secondary, and court-led pathways.

EXECUTION MODEL

RECOVERY IS BUILT INTO THE PROCESS.

Sourcing, diligence, structuring, and servicing are treated as one integrated recovery workflow.

ORIGINATION

Bank, AMC, legal, and servicing channels provide repeat primary access.

SCREENING

Collateral quality, claim priority, and enforcement path are reviewed before full diligence.

STRUCTURING

Documentation is built around control, priority, and recoverability.

REALISATION

Positions are actively managed through negotiation, restructuring, enforcement, or disposal.
