China Private Credit Strategy

Market Context

The perception gap and what it creates.

China is the world's second-largest credit market. Persistent underexposure to it is not a neutral position. It is a portfolio choice that carries its own risk. For institutional investors whose mandates require genuine diversification across global credit markets, the relevant question is not whether to have China exposure, but what form that exposure should take.

The years in which China has been most widely described as difficult or uncertain have historically coincided with the most attractive vintage conditions for properly structured private credit. Complexity that deters less specialized allocators tends to compress competition, preserve pricing, and widen the margin between entry basis and recoverable value.

Not all China exposure is equivalent. The conditions that have caused losses for offshore equity holders or unsecured offshore creditors, including regulatory shifts, developer distress, and onshore enforcement dynamics, are structurally distinct from the conditions facing a first-lien secured onshore creditor with direct PRC enforcement rights. Structure determines outcome.

ShoreVest has operated continuously across China's credit markets since 2004. The firm has navigated multiple regulatory cycles, two NPL waves, and the property correction, and has not recorded a principal loss at the deal level across that period. The narrative around China risk has rarely matched the on-the-ground experience of a disciplined, structure-first approach.

Three sub-strategies. One underwriting standard. Capital is deployed across asset-based lending, asset restructuring, and debt resolutions — each anchored by hard collateral, enforceable legal positioning, and a defined path to recovery. Allocation shifts by cycle.

Risk Controls and Downside Governance

Downside management is structural, not discretionary. Portfolio construction is designed to protect principal first and monetize upside through execution.

Underwriting Controls

  • Conservative entry LTV/CTV bands with stress-tested recovery assumptions.
  • Priority claims, enforceability review, and PRC-law documentation standards.
  • No dependence on refinancing to complete value realization.

Portfolio Controls

  • Diversification by borrower, collateral, vintage, and province.
  • Continuous surveillance from origination through disposition.
  • Multiple predefined exits reduce duration and execution risk.
